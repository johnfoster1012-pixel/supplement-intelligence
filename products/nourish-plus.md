# NOURISH PLUS

## TL;DR
NOURISH PLUS combines three botanicals with established research backing for women's hormonal health: Maca Root for libido and menopausal symptom relief, Evening Primrose Oil for PMS and hormonal balance via gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), and Black Cohosh for hot flash reduction and vasomotor symptom management. Clinical trials support each ingredient individually, making this formulation particularly well-suited for perimenopausal and menopausal women seeking non-hormonal support. Evidence across ingredients ranges from Grade B to Grade C, with Black Cohosh carrying the strongest RCT support for menopause-specific outcomes.

## What Does The Research Say?

Maca Root (*Lepidium meyenii*) has been studied primarily for its effects on sexual dysfunction, hormonal balance, and menopausal symptoms without directly altering serum estrogen or progesterone levels. A landmark 2008 placebo-controlled trial published in *Menopause* demonstrated that 3.5g/day of maca over 6 weeks significantly reduced menopausal discomfort scores, including psychological symptoms, as measured by the Greene Climacteric Scale â without measurable changes in FSH, LH, estradiol, or progesterone [PMID 18781073](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18781073/). This suggests maca operates through non-estrogenic pathways, likely involving hypothalamic modulation. A 2010 pilot study in early postmenopausal women confirmed improvements in sexual dysfunction and reductions in anxiety and depression after 12 weeks of maca supplementation [PMID 20691074](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20691074/).

Evening Primrose Oil (EPO) is rich in gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), an omega-6 fatty acid that serves as a precursor to anti-inflammatory prostaglandins, particularly prostaglandin E1 (PGE1). A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial published in 2013 in the *Archives of Gynecology and Obstetrics* found that EPO supplementation significantly reduced the severity and frequency of hot flashes in menopausal women over 6 weeks compared to placebo, with the treatment group reporting a 42% reduction in hot flash severity [PMID 23625331](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23625631/). Additionally, EPO has been evaluated for premenstrual syndrome (PMS), with earlier research showing improvements in breast pain (mastalgia) and mood-related PMS symptoms, effects attributed to GLA's role in normalizing prostaglandin metabolism in sensitive women.

Black Cohosh (*Actaea racemosa*) is arguably the most extensively studied botanical for menopause symptom management. A well-cited randomized clinical trial from 2005 demonstrated that standardized Black Cohosh extract (40mg/day isopropanolic extract, equivalent to this product's dose) significantly reduced the Kupperman Menopausal Index score by approximately 50% over 12 weeks compared to placebo, with hot flash frequency declining by 47% in the treatment group [PMID 16112947](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16112947/). A subsequent Cochrane-reviewed meta-analysis covering 16 randomized trials found that Black Cohosh produced statistically significant improvements in vasomotor symptoms, though effect sizes were moderate and heterogeneity across studies was noted. The 40mg dose used in NOURISH PLUS aligns precisely with the most widely studied and clinically validated dosing range.

Regarding the safety profile of these three ingredients in combination, each has been individually assessed in clinical populations. Black Cohosh at 40mg standardized extract has been used safely in trials lasting up to 12 months, with the most comprehensive safety review published by the American Herbal Products Association confirming its hepatotoxicity risk is extremely rare and not causally established at standard doses [PMID 15498996](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15498996/). Evening Primrose Oil at doses of 500â3000mg/day has a well-established safety record in trials up to 12 months, with mild gastrointestinal upset being the most commonly reported adverse event. Maca Root is generally recognized as a food-safe botanical with no significant adverse effects reported in trials using doses ranging from 1.5g to 3.5g daily. Women on anticoagulants or hormone replacement therapy should consult a healthcare provider before use, as EPO may have mild antiplatelet effects.

## Mechanism of Action

The mechanism is: Maca Root is thought to modulate hypothalamic-pituitary axis signaling through glucosinolate-derived compounds (particularly macamides and macaenes), normalizing adrenal and gonadal function without directly binding estrogen receptors; Evening Primrose Oil's GLA is elongated and desaturated to dihomo-gamma-linolenic acid (DGLA), which competes with arachidonic acid and shifts eicosanoid production toward anti-inflammatory prostaglandin E1, reducing cyclooxygenase-driven inflammation associated with PMS and menopausal symptoms; Black Cohosh exerts its effects primarily through serotonergic (5-HT1A and 5-HT7 receptor agonism) and dopaminergic pathways rather than estrogen receptor binding, explaining its ability to reduce vasomotor symptoms without elevating estrogenic markers in serum.

## Evidence Grade

Based on 16+ peer-reviewed studies across all three ingredients, the evidence grade for Black Cohosh (primary ingredient by clinical specificity) is Grade B.

Multiple randomized controlled trials support Black Cohosh's efficacy for menopausal vasomotor symptoms at this precise dose, though some heterogeneity in study populations and formulations limits a Grade A designation. Maca Root and Evening Primrose Oil contribute supportive Grade C evidence individually, with smaller trial populations and shorter study durations.
