## Mechanism of Action

The mechanism is: Caffeine competitively antagonizes adenosine A1 and A2A receptors in the central nervous system, disinhibiting dopamine and glutamate neurotransmission to produce heightened arousal, faster reaction times, and improved working memory, with peak effect at plasma concentrations achieved ~45 minutes post-ingestion. ## Evidence Grade

Based on over 50 peer-reviewed studies for caffeine and more than 10 human clinical trials for Lion's Mane, the evidence grade for the V-LOVKAFE formulation is **Grade A for caffeine** and **Grade B for Lion's Mane**.

Caffeine's cognitive effects are supported by multiple large meta-analyses and hundreds of RCTs, representing some of the strongest evidence available in nutritional neuroscience. Lion's Mane earns a Grade B because while existing human trials are well-designed, they are limited in number and tend to use sample sizes under 100 participants; larger Phase III-style trials are still needed to solidify the evidence base across diverse populations.
