## Mechanism of Action

The mechanism is: Marine collagen peptides are hydrolyzed to dipeptides and tripeptides (notably Pro-Hyp and Hyp-Gly) in the gastrointestinal tract, absorbed into systemic circulation, and transported to dermal fibroblasts where they stimulate type I and type III collagen synthesis via TGF-Î² signaling pathways while simultaneously inhibiting matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) that degrade existing collagen. MCT oil is hydrolyzed to medium-chain fatty acids in the gut, transported directly to the liver via the portal circulation, and undergoes rapid beta-oxidation to produce acetyl-CoA and subsequently ketone bodies (beta-hydroxybutyrate and acetoacetate), which serve as alternative energy substrates for the brain and skeletal muscle. Caffeine from Arabica coffee exerts its primary effects through competitive antagonism of adenosine A1 and A2A receptors in the central nervous system, reducing inhibitory adenosine signaling to produce increased dopaminergic and noradrenergic neurotransmission, elevated alertness, and improved cognitive throughput.
