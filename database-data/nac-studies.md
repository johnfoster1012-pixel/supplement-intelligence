# NAC Clinical Studies Summary

## Overview
NAC is a versatile compound with 50+ years of clinical research, serving as a glutathione precursor with established benefits in respiratory health, psychiatric conditions, and liver protection.

## Key Studies

### Respiratory Health (COPD, Bronchitis)

| Study | PMID | Dose | Duration | Key Finding |
|-------|------|------|----------|-------------|
| Zheng et al., 2014 (PANTHEON) | 24477426 | 600mg 2x/day | 1 year | 22% reduction in COPD exacerbations |
| Stey et al., 2000 (meta) | 10905768 | 400-600mg/day | 3-6 months | OR 0.52 for exacerbation reduction |
| Decramer et al., 2005 (BRONCUS) | 15939842 | 600mg/day | 3 years | Reduced exacerbations in non-ICS users |

### Psychiatric Applications

| Study | PMID | Dose | Duration | Key Finding |
|-------|------|------|----------|-------------|
| Berk et al., 2008 | 18534556 | 1g 2x/day | 6 months | Significant MADRS improvement in bipolar depression |
| Grant et al., 2009 | 19564965 | 1.2-2.4g/day | 12 weeks | 56% reduction in trichotillomania symptoms |
| Afshar et al., 2012 | 22867086 | 1.2-2.4g/day | 12 weeks | Significant Y-BOCS reduction in OCD |

### Liver Protection

| Study | PMID | Dose | Duration | Key Finding |
|-------|------|------|----------|-------------|
| Prescott et al., 1979 | 36695 | IV protocol | 20 hours | Standard of care for acetaminophen overdose |
| Nguyen-Khac et al., 2011 | 21527579 | IV + steroids | 1 month | Reduced 1-month mortality in alcoholic hepatitis (8% vs 24%) |

### Fertility

| Study | PMID | Dose | Duration | Key Finding |
|-------|------|------|----------|-------------|
| Safarinejad et al., 2009 | 19091332 | 600mg/day | 3 months | +26% sperm concentration, +18% motility |
| Rizk et al., 2005 | 15929848 | 1.2g/day | 5 days/cycle | Improved ovulation rate 52% vs 18% in PCOS |

## Dosage Summary
- **Respiratory**: 600mg 1-2x/day
- **Psychiatric**: 1,000-2,400mg/day (titrate up from 600mg)
- **Liver Support**: 600-1,200mg/day
- **Fertility**: 600mg (male) to 1,200mg (PCOS)

## Safety Notes
- Excellent safety profile; well-tolerated up to 3,000mg/day
- Common: nausea, GI upset, sulfurous breath odor
- Rare: headache, bronchospasm (inhaled form)
- FDA regulatory status for supplements in flux (2020-2021 warning letters)

## Evidence Grade Rationale
Grade B+ — FDA-approved for acetaminophen overdose (A+); strong respiratory evidence; consistent psychiatric findings requiring more large-scale replication.
