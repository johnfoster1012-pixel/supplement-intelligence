Ashwagandha Clinical Studies

Evidence-based summary of human clinical trials for Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera), covering stress, anxiety, cortisol, testosterone, and athletic performance outcomes.

Evidence Grade: B+Aliases: Withania somnifera, Indian Ginseng, KSM-66, Sensoril

Quick Answer

Ashwagandha is one of the most extensively studied adaptogenic herbs, with over 30 randomized controlled trials demonstrating significant effects on stress reduction, cortisol modulation, anxiety symptoms, and physical performance. The strongest evidence supports its use for stress and anxiety (Cohen's d = 0.76–1.34 in meta-analyses), with consistent 20–30% reductions in serum cortisol. Effects on testosterone and athletic performance are promising but require additional large-scale replication.

Mechanism Overview

Ashwagandha's primary bioactive compounds are withanolides (steroidal lactones), particularly withaferin A and withanolide D. These compounds modulate the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis by:

  • Inhibiting cortisol secretion via GABA-mimetic activity and HPA axis regulation
  • Modulating serotonin (5-HT1A/5-HT2) and GABA receptor signaling
  • Reducing inflammatory markers (NF-κB, IL-6, TNF-α)
  • Supporting mitochondrial function and ATP synthesis
  • Enhancing acetylcholine receptor activity (potential cognitive effects)

Standardization matters: KSM-66 (root extract, 5% withanolides) and Sensoril (root + leaf, 10% withanolides) are the most clinically validated forms.

Dosages Used in Studies

KSM-66 (Root Extract)

  • Stress/anxiety: 300mg 2x/day (600mg total)
  • Sleep: 300mg 2x/day
  • Athletic performance: 300mg 2x/day

Sensoril (Root + Leaf)

  • Stress: 125–250mg 1–2x/day
  • Performance: 300mg 2x/day

Shoden (High Withanolide)

  • Sleep/stress: 120–240mg/day

Generic/Unstandardized

  • Root powder: 3–6g/day
  • Aqueous extract: 500–1000mg/day

Safety & Contraindications

Ashwagandha is generally well-tolerated in clinical trials lasting up to 12 weeks at doses up to 1,250mg/day.

  • Common side effects: Mild GI upset, drowsiness (especially at higher doses)
  • Rare: Thyroid hormone elevation (use caution in hyperthyroidism)
  • Drug interactions: May potentiate sedatives, thyroid medications, immunosuppressants
  • Contraindicated: Pregnancy, autoimmune conditions (may stimulate immune response), surgery within 2 weeks
Note: Recent case reports (2021–2023) have linked ashwagandha to rare liver injury. While causality is uncertain, individuals with liver conditions should consult a healthcare provider.

Evidence Summary

  • Stress/Anxiety: Strong evidence (Grade A) — Multiple RCTs show consistent 30–70% reductions in anxiety scores with 20–30% cortisol reduction
  • Testosterone: Moderate evidence (Grade B) — Multiple RCTs show 10–20% increases in healthy and infertile men; more replication needed in larger samples
  • Athletic Performance: Moderate evidence (Grade B) — Consistent improvements in strength, VO2 max, and recovery in trained individuals
  • Sleep: Moderate evidence (Grade B) — RCTs support improved sleep onset and quality
  • Cognitive Function: Preliminary evidence (Grade C) — Some positive findings, but limited high-quality trials
