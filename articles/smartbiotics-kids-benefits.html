What is SMARTBIOTICS KIDS?

SMARTBIOTICS KIDS is a children's probiotic formula specifically designed for developing gut microbiomes in children aged 2–12. The formula combines Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG at 5 billion CFU — the most extensively studied probiotic strain in pediatric medicine — with Bifidobacterium infantis at 3 billion CFU for infant and child-specific gut colonization, and Prebiotic Inulin at 500mg to selectively nourish these beneficial strains and promote microbiome diversity.

The gut microbiome plays a critical role in children's immune development, digestive health, and even cognitive function through the gut-brain axis. Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG (LGG) is the gold-standard pediatric probiotic, with over 300 clinical trials demonstrating its efficacy in preventing antibiotic-associated diarrhea, reducing the duration of acute gastroenteritis, and supporting immune system maturation. Bifidobacterium infantis is a keystone species in the infant and child gut, essential for proper immune training and pathogen resistance.

This product is part of the Children's Collection by Vital Health Global and is available as child-friendly gummies or powder sachets suitable for mixing into food or beverages.

Research-Backed Clinical Benefits

The multi-ingredient synergy in SMARTBIOTICS KIDS delivers benefits individually supported by peer-reviewed research:

  • Reduced Antibiotic-Associated Diarrhea: LGG is the most evidence-supported probiotic for preventing antibiotic-associated diarrhea in children. A Cochrane systematic review found that LGG reduced the incidence of AAD by approximately 60% when administered alongside antibiotic therapy.
  • Improved Digestive Comfort: Both LGG and B. infantis support healthy digestion by maintaining gut barrier integrity, producing antimicrobial compounds, and competing with pathogenic bacteria for colonization sites. Inulin promotes SCFA production for colonocyte nourishment.
  • Immune System Development: The gut houses approximately 70% of the immune system. LGG has been shown to enhance secretory IgA production, stimulate natural killer cell activity, and support the maturation of gut-associated lymphoid tissue (GALT) in children.
  • Microbiome Establishment: B. infantis is a foundational species in the developing child gut microbiome. Supplementation helps establish a healthy microbial community, particularly important after antibiotic use, cesarean delivery, or formula feeding — all of which can disrupt early microbiome development.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: At what age can children start taking SMARTBIOTICS KIDS?

A: SMARTBIOTICS KIDS is formulated for children aged 2 and older. Both LGG and B. infantis have extensive safety data in pediatric populations from age 2. For infants under 2, consult your pediatrician for age-appropriate probiotic recommendations.

Q: Should I give SMARTBIOTICS KIDS during antibiotic treatment?

A: Yes — this is one of the most evidence-supported uses. LGG has been shown to reduce antibiotic-associated diarrhea by approximately 60% when given alongside antibiotics. Administer SMARTBIOTICS KIDS at least 2 hours apart from the antibiotic dose to maximize probiotic survival.

Q: How long should my child take SMARTBIOTICS KIDS?

A: For general gut health maintenance, daily use is recommended. For antibiotic recovery, continue for at least 2 weeks after completing the antibiotic course. Clinical trials demonstrating immune benefits used supplementation periods of 3–6 months.

Q: Are probiotics safe for children?

A: Yes. LGG and B. infantis are among the most extensively studied probiotic strains in pediatric medicine, with excellent safety profiles across hundreds of clinical trials involving thousands of children. SMARTBIOTICS KIDS uses age-appropriate CFU counts and child-friendly delivery formats.

Q: Can SMARTBIOTICS KIDS help with my child's immune system?

A: Yes. Clinical evidence demonstrates that LGG supplementation enhances secretory IgA production, supports natural killer cell activity, and reduces the incidence and duration of upper respiratory tract infections in children. A meta-analysis (PMID: 26447948) confirmed significant immune benefits of probiotic supplementation in pediatric populations.

People Also Ask

What is the best probiotic for kids?

The most evidence-supported pediatric probiotic is Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG (LGG), with over 300 clinical trials in children. SMARTBIOTICS KIDS combines LGG with Bifidobacterium infantis and prebiotic inulin for comprehensive gut and immune support.

Do kids really need probiotics?

While not all children require probiotic supplementation, specific situations strongly benefit from it: during and after antibiotic treatment, after gastroenteritis, in children born via cesarean section, formula-fed infants transitioning to solid foods, and children with recurrent digestive issues or frequent infections.

How many CFU should a children's probiotic have?

Pediatric clinical trials typically use 1–10 billion CFU per day. SMARTBIOTICS KIDS provides 8 billion total CFU (5B LGG + 3B B. infantis), which is within the clinically effective range demonstrated in pediatric research.

Can probiotics help prevent colds in children?

Yes. Multiple randomized controlled trials have shown that probiotic supplementation (particularly LGG) reduces the incidence, duration, and severity of upper respiratory tract infections in children. A meta-analysis found a significant reduction in the number of days absent from daycare/school due to illness.
