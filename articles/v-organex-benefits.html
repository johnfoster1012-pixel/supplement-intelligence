Key Ingredients & Evidence Grades

Each ingredient in V-ORGANEX was selected based on a review of peer-reviewed clinical literature. Evidence grades are assigned using a standard A–D scale: A = strong, consistent human trial evidence; B = good evidence with some limitations; C = preliminary or mixed findings; D = insufficient evidence.

Evidence Grade: A

N-Acetyl Cysteine (NAC) — 600mg

NAC is arguably the most well-characterized hepatoprotective compound available as a supplement. It serves as the direct precursor to glutathione — the body’s master antioxidant — and is used clinically in hospital settings to treat acetaminophen overdose by rapidly restoring depleted hepatic glutathione stores. At supplemental doses of 600mg, NAC has demonstrated statistically significant reductions in liver enzymes (ALT, AST), improvements in oxidative stress biomarkers, and protective effects against toxic insults to both hepatic and renal tissue.

Beyond hepatoprotection, NAC exhibits mucolytic properties (useful for respiratory health), supports kidney tubular cell protection against nephrotoxins, and has been studied for its role in N-methyl-D-aspartate (NMDA) receptor modulation relevant to neurological health. Its dual role as both an antioxidant precursor and a direct free radical scavenger makes it foundational to any serious organ cleanse formula.

Key Actions: Glutathione precursor • Hepatoprotective • Mucolytic • Oxidative stress reduction • Renal tubular protection

Clinical References: PMID 19489730 • PMID 23912861

Evidence Grade: A

Alpha Lipoic Acid (ALA) — 300mg

Alpha Lipoic Acid is unique among antioxidants because it is both water-soluble and fat-soluble, giving it access to virtually every cell compartment in the body — earning it the designation of “universal antioxidant.” ALA functions as an essential mitochondrial cofactor in oxidative metabolism and directly regenerates other antioxidants including vitamins C, E, and glutathione itself, creating a powerful antioxidant recycling network.

Of particular relevance to detoxification, ALA has demonstrated capacity to chelate heavy metals including mercury, arsenic, and cadmium, facilitating their mobilization and excretion. Clinical trials have shown ALA at 300–600mg/day to improve insulin sensitivity, reduce hepatic fat accumulation, lower inflammatory markers in liver disease, and protect against diabetic nephropathy. Its inclusion at 300mg in V-ORGANEX complements NAC’s glutathione-boosting activity with direct antioxidant and metal-chelating actions.

Key Actions: Universal antioxidant • Mitochondrial cofactor • Heavy metal chelation • Antioxidant recycling • Insulin sensitization

Clinical References: PMID 22228617

Artichoke Leaf Extract (Cynara scolymus) — 400mg

Artichoke Leaf Extract (ALE) is derived from the leaves of Cynara scolymus and is standardized for its content of cynarin and chlorogenic acid — polyphenolic compounds responsible for its hepatoprotective and choleretic (bile-stimulating) effects. Clinical evidence supports ALE for normalizing elevated liver enzymes in individuals with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), reducing total and LDL cholesterol through upregulation of hepatic LDL receptors, and increasing bile flow to support fat digestion and intestinal toxin clearance.

The Grade B designation reflects a solid but not yet extensive body of randomized controlled trial data. Studies at 400–600mg standardized extract daily have consistently shown improvements in hepatic biomarkers with an excellent safety profile. Artichoke also provides meaningful antioxidant activity through its flavonoid content, adding another dimension to V-ORGANEX’s multi-pathway approach to liver support.

Clinical Benefits

1. Liver Enzyme Normalization

Elevated serum liver enzymes — particularly alanine aminotransferase (ALT) and aspartate aminotransferase (AST) — are sensitive markers of hepatocellular stress and damage. Both NAC and Artichoke Leaf Extract have demonstrated statistically significant reductions in these markers in clinical trials. NAC achieves this by replenishing glutathione and quenching reactive oxygen species before they damage hepatocyte membranes and mitochondria. Artichoke Leaf Extract complements this by reducing lipid peroxidation and supporting bile flow, which prevents accumulation of hepatotoxic bile salts. Together, these mechanisms create a synergistic effect on liver enzyme normalization particularly relevant for individuals with fatty liver, alcohol-related hepatic stress, or medication-induced hepatotoxicity.

2. Kidney Filtration Support

The kidneys filter approximately 200 liters of blood daily, exposing their tubular cells to high concentrations of circulating toxins, heavy metals, and oxidized metabolites. NAC has been extensively studied for its nephroprotective properties, particularly in contrast-induced nephropathy and drug-related renal toxicity, where it reduces creatinine elevations and preserves glomerular filtration rate. ALA’s heavy metal chelating capacity further reduces the renal tubular burden from accumulated metals such as cadmium and mercury, which are leading contributors to chronic kidney disease in environmentally exposed populations.

3. Heavy Metal Detoxification

Alpha Lipoic Acid is one of the few natural compounds with clinically documented heavy metal chelation activity. By binding divalent metal cations through its dithiol structure, ALA facilitates mobilization and renal excretion of mercury, arsenic, lead, and cadmium. This activity is significantly enhanced when combined with NAC, whose cysteine thiol groups provide additional binding capacity. For individuals with occupational exposures, high seafood consumption, or urban environmental exposures, this dual-thiol chelation mechanism represents a meaningful detoxification intervention.

4. Oxidative Stress Reduction

Chronic oxidative stress — characterized by an imbalance between reactive oxygen species (ROS) production and antioxidant capacity — is now recognized as a central driver of organ dysfunction, premature aging, metabolic disease, and carcinogenesis. V-ORGANEX addresses oxidative stress at multiple nodes: NAC replenishes the rate-limiting substrate (cysteine) for glutathione synthesis; ALA directly scavenges free radicals and regenerates other antioxidants; and Artichoke Leaf Extract’s polyphenols inhibit lipid peroxidation. This multi-target antioxidant strategy is more comprehensive than any single-ingredient approach.

Mechanism of Action

Understanding how V-ORGANEX works requires a brief overview of the body’s detoxification architecture and how each ingredient interfaces with it.

Phase I and Phase II Hepatic Detoxification

The liver processes fat-soluble toxins through a two-phase enzymatic system. Phase I (cytochrome P450 enzymes) converts toxins into reactive intermediates, and Phase II conjugation reactions (glucuronidation, sulfation, glutathione conjugation) neutralize these intermediates for excretion. This process is highly glutathione-dependent — depletion of glutathione allows Phase I reactive intermediates to accumulate and damage hepatocytes. NAC directly supports Phase II by ensuring glutathione availability. ALA supports both phases by maintaining antioxidant cofactor pools and protecting cytochrome P450 enzymes from oxidative inactivation.

Glutathione Synthesis Pathway

Glutathione (GSH) is synthesized from three amino acids: glycine, glutamate, and cysteine. Cysteine availability is typically the rate-limiting step. NAC provides a bioavailable, stable form of cysteine that readily crosses cell membranes, is converted to cysteine intracellularly, and drives GSH synthesis. At 600mg per serving, V-ORGANEX delivers a clinically meaningful dose of this precursor. ALA further amplifies intracellular GSH levels by reducing oxidized glutathione (GSSG) back to its active form (GSH), effectively extending the functional life of each glutathione molecule produced.

Bile Flow and Enterohepatic Circulation

Artichoke Leaf Extract stimulates hepatic bile production and biliary secretion into the duodenum. Adequate bile flow serves multiple detoxification functions: it emulsifies fat-soluble toxins for intestinal elimination, maintains healthy gut motility that reduces toxin reabsorption, and prevents the stagnation of hepatotoxic bile acids within the liver. By enhancing bile flow, Artichoke Leaf Extract supports a critical downstream step in hepatic detoxification that the antioxidant ingredients alone cannot address.

Lymphatic Support

While less directly studied at the ingredient level, the combination of reduced systemic oxidative stress, improved hepatic clearance, and lower circulating toxin load collectively supports lymphatic function. The lymphatic system is responsible for collecting interstitial fluid, immune surveillance, and returning filtered metabolic waste to central circulation for hepatic and renal processing. By reducing the overall oxidative and toxic burden presented to lymphatic tissues, V-ORGANEX supports this often-overlooked system indirectly but meaningfully.

Safety Profile

V-ORGANEX has a favorable safety profile consistent with its well-characterized ingredient list. Below is a detailed assessment of each component:

N-Acetyl Cysteine (NAC) — 600mg

NAC is one of the most extensively studied supplements in clinical medicine, with decades of human safety data from both pharmaceutical and supplemental use. At 600mg daily, adverse effects are rare. Some individuals experience mild gastrointestinal symptoms (nausea, bloating, or loose stools) particularly when taken on an empty stomach — taking NAC with meals as directed by V-ORGANEX’s dosing protocol significantly mitigates this. At higher doses (>1800mg/day), NAC may theoretically interfere with platelet aggregation. Individuals with cystinuria (a rare metabolic disorder) should avoid NAC. Important: individuals with existing kidney disease should consult a physician, as increased cysteine availability may require monitoring in this population.

Alpha Lipoic Acid (ALA) — 300mg

ALA at 300–600mg/day is well tolerated in clinical trials. Mild gastrointestinal symptoms are the most commonly reported side effect at higher doses. ALA may lower blood glucose levels, which is generally beneficial but warrants monitoring in individuals on insulin or oral hypoglycemic agents. ALA supplementation is also not recommended during thiamine deficiency, as it can exacerbate thiamine-dependent metabolic demands. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare provider before use.

Artichoke Leaf Extract — 400mg

Artichoke Leaf Extract has an excellent safety record at doses up to 640mg standardized extract in clinical trials. Individuals with bile duct obstruction or gallstones should avoid ALE due to its choleretic (bile-stimulating) activity, as increased bile flow may exacerbate these conditions. Artichoke allergy (related to the Asteraceae/Compositae plant family, which includes ragweed, chrysanthemums, and daisies) is a contraindication. Otherwise, ALE is well tolerated with no significant drug interactions reported at supplemental doses.

Dosing Guidelines

The recommended dosing for V-ORGANEX is 2 capsules daily with meals. This protocol has been designed to optimize bioavailability, minimize gastrointestinal side effects, and deliver ingredients at clinically relevant doses within the context of a daily supplement regimen.

Timing Recommendations

Taking V-ORGANEX with meals is important for several reasons. NAC’s gastrointestinal tolerability improves significantly when taken with food. ALA absorption is influenced by the presence of food — while some studies suggest fasted ALA has higher peak plasma levels, the practical tolerability and sustained plasma levels with meals make food co-administration preferable for daily supplementation. Artichoke Leaf Extract’s choleretic activity is most physiologically relevant when bile production is naturally stimulated by meal ingestion.

Duration of Use

V-ORGANEX is suitable for continuous daily use as part of a liver and kidney health maintenance program. For structured detox protocols, a minimum of 4–8 weeks is recommended to observe meaningful improvements in oxidative stress biomarkers and hepatic enzyme profiles, as seen in NAC and ALA clinical trials. Some users cycle the product (e.g., 8 weeks on, 2 weeks off) though there is no clinical requirement for cycling with these ingredients.

Stacking Considerations

V-ORGANEX can be combined with general multivitamins, omega-3 fatty acids, and probiotics without known interactions. Avoid co-administration with other high-dose NAC or ALA supplements to prevent excessive accumulation. If taking medications processed by the liver (CYP450 substrates), consult a pharmacist regarding potential ALA-mediated enzyme interactions, though these are generally minor at supplemental doses.
