The potential of curcumin as a neuroprotective agent has attracted enormous research interest, driven by its ability to cross the blood-brain barrier (a notable achievement for a polyphenol), its multi-target anti-inflammatory and antioxidant mechanisms, and epidemiological observations suggesting that populations with high turmeric consumption may have lower rates of neurological disease. Declining BDNF levels are associated with depression, cognitive decline, and Alzheimer's disease.
