Vitamin B12 — Methylcobalamin (1000 mcg)

Methylcobalamin is the bioactive, neurologically preferred form of Vitamin B12. Unlike cyanocobalamin, it does not require hepatic conversion and crosses the blood-brain barrier more efficiently. B12 is an essential cofactor in the methylation cycle, supporting the synthesis of S-adenosylmethionine (SAMe), DNA repair, and myelin sheath integrity.

For energy metabolism specifically, B12 participates in the conversion of homocysteine to methionine and supports succinyl-CoA formation—a critical step in the Krebs cycle that feeds directly into mitochondrial ATP production. B12 deficiency is strongly linked to macrocytic anaemia, fatigue, peripheral neuropathy, and cognitive decline. Repleting or optimising B12 levels with a bioavailable form such as methylcobalamin has robust evidence for reversing fatigue and neurological symptoms.

At 1000 mcg, this dose is well above the RDA of 2.4 mcg but is commonly used in clinical and functional medicine settings, particularly for individuals with absorption challenges (e.g., those on metformin, proton-pump inhibitors, or with low intrinsic factor).

Key mechanisms: Cofactor in Krebs cycle succinyl-CoA pathway • Methionine synthesis via methylation • Red blood cell maturation • Neuronal myelination and repair

Evidence Grade: B

Rhodiola Rosea (200 mg)

Rhodiola Rosea is an adaptogenic herb with a well-documented history of use in traditional Russian and Scandinavian medicine for combating fatigue in harsh environmental conditions. Its primary bioactive constituents—rosavin, salidroside, and tyrosol—modulate the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis and the sympatho-adrenal system, reducing cortisol output during stress while preserving catecholamine availability.

Multiple randomised controlled trials have evaluated Rhodiola in populations experiencing stress-related fatigue, burn-out syndrome, and physical exhaustion. A 2010 placebo-controlled trial (PMID 20378318) demonstrated significant reductions in fatigue and significant improvements in attention, speed, and accuracy on cognitive tasks in students under examination stress. A 2018 review (PMID 29325481) further corroborated its role in improving physical endurance and mental performance, noting its favourable safety profile.

The 200 mg dose in V-NRGY aligns with the lower end of clinically studied ranges (200–680 mg standardised extract). The degree of benefit will vary based on extract standardisation (typically 3% rosavin / 1% salidroside) and individual stress burden. Evidence Grade B reflects strong but not yet fully replicated high-quality trial data across diverse populations.

Key mechanisms: HPA axis cortisol modulation • Monoamine oxidase (MAO) inhibition (mild) • Nitric oxide upregulation • Neuroprotective antioxidant activity • Heat-shock protein expression

Evidence Grade: A

CoQ10 — Ubiquinol (100 mg)

Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10) is an endogenously synthesised lipid-soluble molecule that serves as an essential electron carrier in the mitochondrial electron transport chain (ETC). Ubiquinol is the fully reduced, biologically active form of CoQ10—superior in oral bioavailability compared to ubiquinone, particularly in older adults and those with impaired conversion capacity.

CoQ10 is concentrated in tissues with the highest energetic demands: the heart, liver, kidneys, and skeletal muscle. It accepts electrons from Complexes I and II of the ETC and donates them to Complex III, driving the proton gradient that powers ATP synthase (Complex V). CoQ10 additionally functions as a potent lipid-phase antioxidant, protecting mitochondrial membranes and circulating LDL from oxidative damage.

A landmark meta-analysis (PMID 25386977) demonstrated that CoQ10 supplementation significantly reduces exercise-induced muscle damage markers and improves physical performance metrics. Importantly, statins deplete endogenous CoQ10, making supplementation particularly relevant for this population.

The 100 mg ubiquinol dose in V-NRGY is clinically meaningful and reflects standard supplementation protocols documented in the literature. Ubiquinol form ensures enhanced systemic absorption compared to standard ubiquinone products.

Key mechanisms: Mitochondrial ETC electron shuttling (Complexes I–III) • ATP synthase proton gradient support • Lipid-phase antioxidant protection • Mitochondrial membrane integrity • Cardioprotective and neuroprotective effects

Dosing Guidelines

Recommended Dose: 2 capsules daily with food

Timing Guidance: Morning or early afternoon is ideal. Rhodiola Rosea has mild activating properties and may disrupt sleep if taken in the evening. Taking with food improves CoQ10 absorption (fat-soluble compound).

Population Considerations:

  • Athletes: Take 30–60 minutes before training or competition for Rhodiola-mediated performance benefits. Consistent daily use is required for full CoQ10 tissue saturation (typically 2–4 weeks).
  • Professionals / High-Performers: Daily morning use establishes a consistent adaptogenic baseline. Expect progressive improvements in energy resilience over 2–4 weeks of consistent use.
  • B12-Deficient Individuals: The 1000 mcg methylcobalamin dose is appropriate for repletion. Serum B12 and homocysteine testing is recommended for baseline assessment.
  • Older Adults (50+): CoQ10 biosynthesis declines with age; ubiquinol form ensures maximal bioavailability.

Do Not Exceed the recommended dose unless directed by a healthcare provider. Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is V-NRGY a stimulant-free product?

Yes. V-NRGY does not contain caffeine, guarana, synephrine, or any other conventional stimulant. Its energy-supporting effects derive from mitochondrial cofactors (B12, CoQ10) and HPA-axis adaptogens (Rhodiola Rosea). Some users sensitive to Rhodiola’s activating properties may notice a mild alertness effect; taking it in the morning mitigates any sleep-related concerns.

How long before I notice results?

B12 effects may be noticed within days, particularly in individuals with suboptimal baseline levels. Rhodiola Rosea demonstrates acute fatigue-reducing effects within a single dose in some studies, though maximal adaptogenic benefit accrues over 2–4 weeks of consistent use. CoQ10 tissue saturation typically requires 2–4 weeks. Most users report noticeable improvements in energy and mental clarity within 2–3 weeks of daily supplementation.

Can I take V-NRGY with other supplements?

V-NRGY is generally compatible with most supplement stacks. It is particularly complementary with magnesium (supports ATP utilisation), omega-3s (anti-inflammatory synergy with CoQ10), and adaptogenic stacks. Caution is advised with other MAO-modulating supplements or herbs when combined with Rhodiola. Always review your full supplement and medication list with a healthcare provider.

Is V-NRGY suitable for vegetarians or vegans?

Methylcobalamin B12 is commonly synthesised via fermentation and is vegan-compatible. Rhodiola Rosea is plant-derived. CoQ10 can be produced through fermentation processes suitable for vegans. Capsule shell composition should be verified directly with Vital Health Global to confirm suitability for strict vegetarian/vegan consumers.

Why is CoQ10 listed as ubiquinol rather than ubiquinone?

Ubiquinol is the reduced, electron-rich form of CoQ10 and is the predominant form found in healthy human plasma. Research consistently demonstrates superior oral bioavailability for ubiquinol compared to ubiquinone, particularly in populations over 40 where endogenous conversion of ubiquinone to ubiquinol becomes less efficient. The use of ubiquinol in V-NRGY reflects a deliberate formulation choice to maximise clinical impact.
