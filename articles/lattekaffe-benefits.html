Safety Profile

LATTEKAFFE safety summary: Well-tolerated; suitable for daily use

  • Arabica Coffee (1500mg): Contains moderate caffeine. Well-tolerated by most adults. Avoid if caffeine-sensitive.
  • Collagen Peptides (2500mg): Excellent safety profile. Derived from animal sources — not suitable for vegans. Rare allergic reactions in individuals with fish/shellfish allergies if marine-sourced.
  • Inulin (1000mg): Generally well-tolerated. May cause mild bloating or gas in individuals not accustomed to prebiotic fiber — start with half serving if sensitive.

Contraindications:

  • Contains caffeine — avoid within 6 hours of bedtime
  • Not suitable for vegans (contains collagen from animal sources)
  • Individuals with fish/shellfish allergies should verify collagen source
  • Start with half serving if sensitive to prebiotic fiber

People Also Ask

Is collagen coffee effective?

Yes. Hydrolyzed collagen peptides are well-absorbed orally and have demonstrated benefits for skin elasticity and hydration in randomized controlled trials (PMID: 27852613). Adding collagen to coffee is an efficient way to incorporate daily collagen supplementation into an existing routine.

What is inulin and why is it in coffee?

Inulin is a prebiotic fiber that selectively feeds beneficial gut bacteria (particularly Bifidobacteria). Adding it to coffee provides gut microbiome support with every cup, promoting digestive health, immune function, and SCFA production without changing the taste significantly.

How much collagen should I take daily?

Clinical trials showing skin benefits typically use 2,500–10,000mg/day of hydrolyzed collagen peptides. LATTEKAFFE provides 2,500mg per serving, which is the minimum effective dose demonstrated in research. For enhanced benefits, it can be combined with additional collagen supplementation.

Does coffee with collagen help skin?

The collagen peptides in functional coffee products like LATTEKAFFE provide bioavailable amino acids (glycine, proline, hydroxyproline) that support skin collagen synthesis. Clinical evidence supports improvements in skin elasticity and hydration with daily collagen peptide intake over 4–12 weeks.
