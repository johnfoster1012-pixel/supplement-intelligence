Evidence Grade: A

Arabica Coffee — 1500mg

Primary mechanisms: Adenosine receptor antagonism; catecholamine release; phosphodiesterase inhibition

Arabica coffee provides caffeine, the world’s most thoroughly studied psychoactive compound. At its core, caffeine competitively blocks adenosine A1 and A2A receptors in the brain, preventing the accumulation of adenosine-driven fatigue signals. This promotes wakefulness, elevates dopamine activity, and enhances norepinephrine release — together producing the well-recognised improvements in alertness, reaction time, and mood.

The 1500mg arabica dose in LATTEKAFFE delivers a moderate, functional caffeine level that supports energy without excessive stimulation. Arabica varieties are prized for their lower robustness-caffeine content and smoother flavour profile compared to robusta, making them particularly suited to a wellness latte format where palatability and daily tolerability are priorities. Beyond caffeine, arabica coffee contains chlorogenic acids, which have antioxidant and modest glucose-regulatory properties.

Grade A evidence: Hundreds of randomised controlled trials (RCTs) and large-scale meta-analyses confirm caffeine’s cognitive and ergogenic benefits. PMID: 23871506

Evidence Grade: B

Collagen Peptides — 2500mg

Primary mechanisms: Gut epithelial barrier reinforcement; dermal matrix stimulation; synovial fluid support

Hydrolysed collagen peptides are short-chain amino acid sequences â predominantly hydroxyproline, proline, and glycine â produced by enzymatic hydrolysis of native collagen. This processing dramatically improves bioavailability compared to intact collagen proteins, with specific di- and tripeptides (notably Pro-Hyp) shown to be absorbed intact and bioactive in human studies.

In the gut, collagen-derived amino acids provide substrate for tight-junction protein synthesis and intestinal epithelial cell renewal, supporting barrier function. This is especially relevant when combined with prebiotic fibre, as both pathways converge on reducing gut permeability. At the dermal level, collagen peptides stimulate fibroblast activity and extracellular matrix production, contributing to improvements in skin hydration, elasticity, and the reduction of fine lines observed in clinical trials. Joint cartilage and synovial fluid also benefit from collagen-derived amino acids as structural precursors.

The 2500mg dose falls within the range used in clinical studies demonstrating measurable outcomes, though optimal dosing varies by target tissue. Grade B reflects good human RCT evidence with some heterogeneity across outcome measures and populations.

Evidence Grade: A

Inulin (Prebiotic Fibre) — 1000mg

Primary mechanisms: Bifidogenic stimulation; short-chain fatty acid (SCFA) production; gut microbiome diversity enhancement

Inulin is a naturally occurring fructan fibre found in chicory root, Jerusalem artichoke, and other plants. As a non-digestible oligosaccharide, it passes intact through the upper gastrointestinal tract and reaches the colon, where it is selectively fermented by beneficial bacteria, most notably Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus species.

This selective fermentation â the bifidogenic effect â results in significant increases in gut microbiome diversity and beneficial bacterial abundance, alongside the production of short-chain fatty acids (acetate, propionate, butyrate). Butyrate, in particular, is the preferred energy source for colonocytes (colon lining cells) and plays a key role in maintaining intestinal barrier integrity, reducing gut inflammation, and modulating immune signalling. Propionate crosses to the liver and contributes to satiety and glycaemic regulation, while acetate has systemic anti-inflammatory effects.

At 1000mg daily, inulin provides a clinically meaningful prebiotic dose with a well-established safety profile. Grade A reflects the robust and consistent evidence base across multiple human trials and mechanistic studies.

Grade A evidence: PMID: 17761020

Mechanism of Action

LATTEKAFFE operates across three distinct but interconnected biological systems, creating a functional synergy that exceeds the sum of its individual parts.

1. Central Nervous System: Adenosine Antagonism

Caffeine from arabica coffee rapidly crosses the blood-brain barrier and competes with adenosine for binding to A1 and A2A receptors. Adenosine normally accumulates during waking hours and drives the homeostatic pressure for sleep. By blocking these receptors, caffeine halts this accumulation, elevates dopaminergic tone in reward circuits, and potentiates noradrenergic activity in the locus coeruleus — collectively producing wakefulness, improved working memory, enhanced reaction speed, and elevated mood.

2. Gut-Microbiome Axis: Prebiotic Fermentation and SCFA Production

Inulin undergoes anaerobic fermentation in the distal small intestine and colon. Bifidobacteria and Lactobacillus species preferentially metabolise inulin’s fructan chains, producing acetate, propionate, and butyrate as primary fermentation products. Butyrate is taken up directly by colonocytes as a primary fuel source and activates GPR41/43 receptors on enteroendocrine cells, triggering PYY and GLP-1 release (satiety hormones). It also activates histone deacetylase inhibition pathways that reduce colonocyte inflammation and support tight-junction protein expression. Propionate is transported to the liver via the portal vein, where it modulates gluconeogenesis and systemic satiety signalling.

3. Extracellular Matrix Remodelling: Collagen Peptide Bioactivity

After intestinal absorption, collagen-derived dipeptides (especially Pro-Hyp and Hyp-Gly) accumulate in skin, cartilage, and other connective tissues. In dermal fibroblasts, these peptides act as partial agonists on specific receptors linked to TGF-β signalling, stimulating endogenous collagen and hyaluronic acid synthesis. In joint cartilage, they support proteoglycan and type II collagen production by chondrocytes. In the gut epithelium, glycine-rich collagen amino acids are preferentially incorporated into tight-junction proteins (claudin, occludin, ZO-1), reinforcing the physical barrier between luminal contents and systemic circulation.

Synergistic Interactions

The convergence of inulin and collagen on gut barrier function is particularly noteworthy. Inulin-driven butyrate production upregulates tight-junction gene expression, while collagen amino acids supply the raw structural material for those same proteins — a biochemical partnership that may produce greater barrier integrity than either ingredient alone. Additionally, chlorogenic acids from arabica coffee have prebiotic-like effects on gut microbiota composition and may further complement inulin’s bifidogenic activity.

Special Populations

  • Pregnancy & lactation: Moderate caffeine (<200mg/day total from all sources) is generally considered safe during pregnancy; consult a healthcare provider before use.
  • Children and adolescents: Not recommended due to caffeine content.
  • Caffeine sensitivity: Individuals sensitive to caffeine should begin with half a sachet to assess tolerance.
  • FODMAP sensitivity / IBS: Inulin is a fermentable oligosaccharide and may exacerbate symptoms in individuals with fructan intolerance. Start with lower doses or consult a dietitian.
  • Collagen source: Users with dietary restrictions (halal, kosher, vegan) should verify the collagen source with the manufacturer.

No clinically significant drug interactions have been identified at the doses provided. Caffeine may mildly enhance the effects of stimulant medications and interact with adenosine-based drugs; individuals on prescription medications should consult their physician.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is LATTEKAFFE suitable for vegetarians or vegans?

The collagen peptides in LATTEKAFFE are derived from animal sources (typically bovine or marine). This means LATTEKAFFE is not suitable for vegans. Vegetarians who avoid animal-derived proteins should check with the manufacturer regarding the specific collagen source. All other ingredients (arabica coffee, inulin) are plant-derived.

How does LATTEKAFFE compare to plain coffee?

Plain coffee provides caffeine and antioxidants but lacks the gut-supportive and structural benefits of collagen peptides and prebiotic inulin. LATTEKAFFE retains all the sensory and energetic qualities of a quality latte while adding meaningful functional nutrition in a single, convenient format — making it an upgrade rather than a replacement for coffee drinkers seeking broader wellness support.

Can I have LATTEKAFFE on an empty stomach?

Yes, LATTEKAFFE can be consumed on an empty stomach. The collagen peptides and inulin may actually support gut lining resilience, which can ease any gastric sensitivity to caffeine in some individuals. However, those with known caffeine sensitivity or gastric reflux may prefer to take it with food.

How long before I notice results?

Energy benefits are felt within 30–60 minutes of the first serving. Gut microbiome and satiety improvements typically become noticeable within 2–4 weeks of daily use. Skin elasticity and joint comfort improvements generally require 4–8 weeks, with peak collagen-related benefits at 8–12 weeks of consistent supplementation.

Is LATTEKAFFE safe to use every day long-term?

Yes. All three ingredients are well-tolerated for long-term daily use in healthy adults. Inulin has been consumed as a regular dietary component for millennia in high-fibre diets. Collagen peptide studies have been conducted over periods of up to 12 months without significant adverse events. Moderate caffeine from arabica coffee is considered safe for daily consumption in healthy adults.
