What is V-TEDETOX?

V-TEDETOX is a premium herbal detox tea supplement formulated to support the body's natural detoxification pathways — particularly the liver, kidneys, and digestive tract. Unlike harsh laxative-based cleanse products, V-TEDETOX takes a gentler, more physiologically sound approach: supplying concentrated botanical extracts that enhance the organs already responsible for filtering and eliminating metabolic waste.

The formula centers on three anchor ingredients with well-documented clinical evidence: dandelion root (Taraxacum officinale), milk thistle seed (Silybum marianum), and ginger rhizome (Zingiber officinale). These are complemented by supporting botanicals that address inflammation, fluid retention, and digestive discomfort — creating a synergistic blend that is both effective and well-tolerated for daily consumption.

The product is positioned within the adult supplement category and is suitable for individuals seeking to improve liver resilience, reduce bloating, support healthy digestion, and feel lighter and more energized as part of an overall wellness routine.

ð« 1. Liver Support and Detoxification

The liver is the body's central detoxification organ, processing hundreds of endogenous and exogenous compounds daily. Silymarin — the bioactive flavonolignan complex extracted from milk thistle seeds — is one of the most extensively studied natural hepatoprotective agents. It exerts its effects through multiple mechanisms: scavenging reactive oxygen species (ROS), stabilizing hepatocyte membranes, inhibiting NF-ÎºB-mediated inflammatory signaling, and stimulating ribosomal RNA synthesis to promote liver cell regeneration.

A 2020 randomized controlled trial published in Phytomedicine found that participants with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) who supplemented with silymarin experienced statistically significant reductions in alanine aminotransferase (ALT) and aspartate aminotransferase (AST) — two primary biomarkers of liver stress — compared to placebo over a 12-week intervention period. Hepatic steatosis scores also improved meaningfully in the treatment group.
