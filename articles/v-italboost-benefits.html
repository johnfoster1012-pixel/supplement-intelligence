Evidence Grade: B

Elderberry Extract (Sambucus nigra) — 500mg

Mechanism: Elderberry is one of the most widely studied botanical immune agents, with its primary bioactive compounds being anthocyanin flavonoids, particularly cyanidin-3-glucoside and cyanidin-3-sambubioside. These compounds exert direct antiviral activity by binding to and interfering with viral surface proteins, preventing viral entry into host cells. In addition to antiviral activity, elderberry extract modulates cytokine production — stimulating beneficial immune-activating cytokines such as interleukin-6 and tumor necrosis factor-alpha, which coordinate the early immune response and help clear viral infections more rapidly. Meta-analytic clinical data shows that elderberry supplementation meaningfully reduces the duration and severity of upper respiratory tract infections.

Clinical Evidence (PubMed ID): 28198157

Evidence Grade: B

Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid) — 150 mg

Mechanism: Vitamin C is an essential micronutrient that plays multiple non-redundant roles in immune function. It is required for the chemotaxis, phagocytosis, and oxidant-generating capacity of neutrophils — the immune system's first responders to infection. Vitamin C also supports the differentiation and proliferation of B and T lymphocytes, facilitates antibody synthesis, and acts as a potent water-soluble antioxidant that protects immune cells from oxidative damage generated during active immune responses. Vitamin C levels are rapidly depleted during infection and physiological stress, making supplementation particularly relevant during periods of heightened immune demand. The 150 mg dose in V-ITALBOOST reflects a clinically meaningful and well-tolerated level for daily immune maintenance.

Clinical Evidence (PubMed ID): 29099763

Evidence Grade: B

Zinc — 10mg

Mechanism: Zinc is an essential trace mineral and cofactor for over 300 enzymatic reactions in the human body, including many that are central to immune function. It is required for the development, maturation, and activation of T-lymphocytes, the adaptive immune cells responsible for recognizing and eliminating specific pathogens. Zinc also possesses direct antiviral properties — it inhibits viral RNA polymerase activity and has been shown to reduce viral replication when present in adequate concentrations within cells. Additionally, zinc is critical for wound healing and mucosal barrier integrity, supporting the body's first line of physical defense against infection. Zinc deficiency is associated with significantly impaired immune function, making adequate daily intake a foundational requirement for immune resilience.

Clinical Evidence (PubMed ID): 25726516

Safety Profile

V-ITALBOOST has a well-tolerated overall safety profile based on the clinical literature for all three ingredients. Each compound is widely used, well characterized from a safety perspective, and appropriate for the general adult population under normal conditions.

  • Elderberry Extract (500mg): Generally very well tolerated at standard supplemental doses. Raw elderberries contain sambunigrin (a cyanogenic glycoside) that can cause nausea, but commercial extracts are processed to eliminate this compound. Most reported side effects are mild GI complaints. Elderberry is not recommended for individuals with autoimmune conditions (such as lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, or multiple sclerosis) due to its cytokine-stimulating activity, which may theoretically amplify autoimmune responses.
  • Vitamin C (150 mg): At the 500mg dose in V-ITALBOOST, Vitamin C is extremely safe and well tolerated by the vast majority of adults. Higher doses may cause GI upset in sensitive individuals; this risk is minimal at 150 mg. Taking with food further reduces any potential for GI discomfort.
  • Zinc (10mg): The 10mg dose is well within safe supplemental ranges (the Tolerable Upper Intake Level for zinc is 40mg/day for adults). Zinc is very well tolerated at this dose. Long-term use at high doses (above 40mg/day) can interfere with copper absorption, but this is not a concern at the 10mg level in V-ITALBOOST.

Contraindications & Precautions:

  • Elderberry is not recommended for individuals with autoimmune conditions or those on immunosuppressant medications without medical supervision
  • Consult your healthcare provider before use if pregnant or nursing
  • Individuals taking anticoagulants or immunomodulatory medications should discuss elderberry supplementation with their physician
  • Not intended for use by individuals under 18 years of age without medical guidance

People Also Ask

Does elderberry really help with colds and flu?

Yes. A 2016 meta-analysis (PMID 28198157) pooling data from multiple randomized controlled trials found that elderberry supplementation significantly reduced the duration and severity of upper respiratory infections. The anthocyanin flavonoids in elderberry inhibit viral entry into host cells and stimulate immune-activating cytokines, providing a dual antiviral and immunomodulatory mechanism of action.

What does zinc do for the immune system?

Zinc is essential for the development, differentiation, and activation of T-lymphocytes — the adaptive immune cells responsible for recognizing and eliminating specific viruses and bacteria. It also inhibits viral RNA polymerase activity (reducing viral replication), supports mucosal barrier integrity as the body's first physical defense, and plays a role in wound healing and recovery. Even mild zinc deficiency significantly impairs immune competence.

How does Vitamin C support the immune system?

Vitamin C enhances multiple aspects of immune function: it improves the migration of neutrophils to sites of infection (chemotaxis), boosts their ability to engulf and destroy pathogens (phagocytosis), supports lymphocyte proliferation and differentiation, facilitates antibody synthesis, and acts as an antioxidant that protects immune cells from oxidative damage generated during active responses. Vitamin C is rapidly depleted during illness and stress, making supplementation particularly valuable during immune challenges.

Can I take elderberry and zinc together?

Absolutely. Elderberry and zinc address different aspects of immune function — elderberry provides antiviral botanical activity and cytokine modulation, while zinc ensures the adaptive immune system has the foundational mineral cofactors it needs for T-cell function and antiviral activity. They are complementary rather than overlapping, making the combination more comprehensive than either ingredient alone.

What is the best immune supplement for post-illness recovery?

Post-illness recovery involves both resolving residual immune activation and replenishing nutrients depleted during the immune response. V-ITALBOOST addresses both: Vitamin C and zinc are commonly depleted during infection and need to be restored, while elderberry's antioxidant flavonoids help clear oxidative stress generated during immune activity. Together, they support faster return to normal energy and vitality following illness.

Is elderberry safe to take every day?

For generally healthy adults without autoimmune conditions, elderberry extract at standard supplemental doses (up to 500mg daily) is considered safe for daily preventive use based on available clinical data. It is not recommended for individuals with autoimmune diseases or those on immunosuppressant medications, as its cytokine-stimulating activity may be contraindicated in those contexts.
